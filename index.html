The Physics of Computers

by Michael Stirchak

How do Computers Work?

  • Modern computers read in electric signals as either 1's(on) or 0's(off).
  • Transistors are uniquely equipped to read in these signals, which are called bits.
  • By combining transistors in a circuit, we can construct boolean logic gates which can perform operations on bits.

MOSFETs

  • MOSFET: Metal Oxide Semiconductor Field-Effect Transistor.
  • Consists of drain, gate, and source outlets.
  • A positive voltage across the gate determines if the current from the drain passes to the source


MOSFET Architecture

  • drain and source feed into doped (n-type) regions of the semiconductor, and between them is a p-type region
  • There exists a depletion layer between the n- and p-type regions which repels current.
  • When a positive voltage is across the gate, a conduction channel is formed from drain to source.


Example: MOSFET Switch

  • When Switch is open, no voltage across gate. No current flows from drain to source, and lamp (M) is off.
  • When switch is closed, the voltage across the gate allows current to flow. Lamp turns on.
